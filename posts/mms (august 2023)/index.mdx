I just took CMU's Mathematical Maturity Survey and I feel like I did so horribly. The test breakdown was 10 multiple choice AMC-esque questions, 6 AIME-esque questions, 6 discrete math proofs, and 6 linear algebra proofs. On each of the sections, I think I got an 8, 4, 3, and 5.5. The score itself doesn't seem to evaluate too horrible, but it is worth at least noting that the proofs were worth so much more than the rest of the test. 

I can't do anything about it now, so it's whatever. I really wanted to test out of at least one of 15-151 or 21-242, but I don't know how likely that is now.
